About

I am currently pursuing a Ph.D. in Applied Economics at Ibn Zohr University, where I also actively contribute as a Graduate Teaching Assistant. My academic journey is characterized by a profound interest in the dynamic field of macroeconomics, with a primary focus on macro-finance, monetary policy, and applied macroeconometrics.

One of my key research passions revolves around the relationship between macroeconomics and finance, known as macro-finance. Within this exciting realm, I am particularly drawn to exploring Dynamic Stochastic General Equilibrium (DSGE) models and the impact of financial frictions on economic dynamics.

Teaching

  • DSGE modeling
  • Business cycle and Macroeconomic stabilization policies
